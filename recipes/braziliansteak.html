Brazilian Steak

  1. 1.5-2 pounds picanha steak (ask your butcher for a good quality cut)
  2. 1 tablespoon olive oil
  3. 1 teaspoon salt
  4. 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  5. 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  6. 1/4 teaspoon onion powder

Instructions:
Prepare the steak: Remove any excess fat from the picanha, leaving the cap intact. Pat the steak dry with paper towels. Season: Rub the olive oil, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder over the entire surface of the steak. Grill or pan-sear: Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Grill the steak for about 10-12 minutes per side, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness. Pan-sear: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the steak and cook for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness. Rest: Allow the steak to rest for about 5-10 minutes before slicing. This will help the juices redistribute. Serving Suggestion:
Slice the picanha against the grain into thin slices. Serve with chimichurri sauce, farofa (a Brazilian crumbly side dish), and your favorite grilled vegetables. Note: For a more authentic Brazilian experience, consider using a churrascaria-style grill that allows you to cook the steak over an open flame.
